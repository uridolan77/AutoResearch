## **4.0.1 The Chapter's Problem**

Chapters 1-3 assembled three results. The triadic grammar \- variation, encounter, mediation \- established the irreducible compositional minimum for any stratum that satisfies the Stratogonic Principle (Ch. 1 §1.3-1.4). The Landauer Floor and the Synthesis Lemma established that every maintained constraint-architecture carries a non-zero dissipative cost, and that the passage from Shannon-syntactic to Landauer-thermodynamic to semantic-governance information is itself a stratogonic achievement, not a free abstraction (Ch. 2 §2.4-2.6). The beaker's three regimes \- supersaturated metastability, propagating front, depleted lattice \- demonstrated that a constitutive-stratogonic boundary is crossed when stabilised aspect-selection, non-zero burn rate, and constraint-closure converge at a single site (Ch. 3 §3.2-3.3). What those chapters did not do is show where this grammar *came from*: why the triadic minimum recurs as a structural demand across the history of immanentist philosophy, or how eight independent philosophical programmes each approached it and each fell short at a precisely diagnosable point.

That is this chapter's problem. It is a genealogy, not a survey. Eight philosophers \- Spinoza, Leibniz, Peirce, Nietzsche, Bergson, Whitehead, Simondon, Deleuze \- are structurally raided: their contributions to the triadic grammar extracted while their metaphysical residues are refused. The criterion of legitimacy is performance at the sodium acetate front, not provenance in the philosophical canon. Each thinker's apparatus is diagnosed by a structural number (σ) recording which of the three compositional functions it secures and which it outsources to a transcendent register. Each residue is mapped onto the Demon-class error taxonomy of Ch. 2 §2.8.7: every residue installs or maintains a distinction without entering it in the dissipative ledger. The demonstration is testable: any system achieving compositional openness with fewer than three functions, or any maintained constraint-architecture shown to require no expenditure, would break it.

The sodium acetate beaker is to this chapter what the planarian is to Ch. 1 and the Szilard engine to Ch. 2\. Every operator introduced below must earn its keep at the beaker \- not as illustration but as ordeal. Where the beaker cannot instantiate a claim, the claim is marked as a debt, not entered as a result.
