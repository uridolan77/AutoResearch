## **4.5 The Lattice Complete**

The lattice is sealed (Spinoza), furnished (Leibniz), historicised (Peirce), driven (Nietzsche), temporalised (Bergson), discretised (Whitehead), propagated (Simondon), and mapped (Deleuze). Eight operators interlock \- closure, generative law, evolutionary constraint, agonistic encounter, irreversible duration, concrescent occasion, transductive propagation, virtual topology \- not as a completed architecture but as the crystallographic lattice within which three problems, suppressed throughout the genealogy, can finally be posed with the precision their difficulty demands.

Its structural lesson is one sentence long: wherever Thirdness is not explicitly secured and metabolically maintained, a transcendent supplement reappears to cover the cost \- whether as God, Will-as-primitive, élan vital, Eternal Objects, Apeironic preindividual, or transcendental virtual. The recurrence is not accidental but structurally necessary: the problem of compositional governance demands triadic resolution, and every attempt to secure it with fewer than three functions \- or to outsource one function to a transcendent register \- generates the residue the next operator must metabolise. Each residue was diagnosed as a Demon-class error (Ch. 2 §2.8.7): an operator installing or maintaining a distinction without entering it in the dissipative ledger, thereby violating one or more Stratogonic conditions.

The demonstration is testable at every subsequent scale. It rests on eight case studies and a single physical ordeal, and it is refutable by a counterexample: any system achieving compositional openness with fewer than three functions, or any maintained constraint-architecture shown to require no expenditure, would break it.
