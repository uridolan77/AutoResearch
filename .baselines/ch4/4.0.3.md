## **4.0.3 Inherited Results**

This chapter inherits and presupposes \- does not re-derive \- the following:

* The triadic grammar and structural-number diagnostic (Ch. 1 §1.3-1.4). Variation, encounter, and mediation are the irreducible compositional minimum. The formal backbone is Peirce's Reduction Thesis, proven by Burch (1991) and Hereth Correia & Pöschel (2006): triadic relations are irreducible to dyadic ones; all higher-arity relations reduce to triadic composition. The structural number σ records which of the three functions a given apparatus secures.  
* The Synthesis Lemma and the Landauer Floor (Ch. 2 §2.4-2.6). Every maintained constraint-architecture carries a non-zero dissipative cost. The Landauer Floor \- $k\_B T \\ln 2$ per bit-erasure \- is the minimum thermodynamic receipt for any operation that performs selection among alternatives. An operator that governs at zero thermodynamic cost is not an immanent resource; it is a transcendent supplement. The Demon-class error taxonomy (Ch. 2 §2.8.7) established that every rival ontology harbours a version of this failure: each installs or maintains a distinction without entering it in the dissipative ledger.  
* The Stratogonic Principle (Ch. 3 §3.2). A constitutive-stratogonic boundary is crossed when three conditions converge: (i) stabilised aspect-selection \- the system selects among competing configurations and that selection persists; (ii) non-zero burn rate \- the selection costs real gradients, $dS\_i/dt \> 0$; (iii) constraint-closure \- the constraint-architecture channels subsequent individuations without requiring external supplementation. Wherever any of these three conditions is absent, the boundary is not crossed and the stratum is not constituted.  
* The beaker's three regimes (Ch. 3 §3.2-3.3). Supersaturated metastability (the charged, undifferentiated field), propagating crystallisation front (the transductive advance through the domain), and depleted lattice with defect signature (the constituted individual carrying its formation-history as operative constraint). The cessation-test (Ch. 3 §3.5): remove the condition and check whether the stratum degrades. The beaker passes: raise the temperature above the dissolution threshold and the lattice collapses \- governance was thermodynamically maintained, not logically entailed.  
* The Shannon-Landauer-Semantic hierarchy (Ch. 3 §3.4). Shannon information (syntactic channel capacity) is necessary but not sufficient for Landauer-thermodynamic information (physically irreversible bit-operations); Landauer-thermodynamic information is necessary but not sufficient for semantic information (governance-bearing constraint that channels subsequent individuation). This hierarchy will be deployed explicitly when diagnosing Simondon's information-as-disparation-resolution and Deleuze's virtual topology.
