## **4.1 Axis I \- House: Boundary & Individuality | Spinoza & Leibniz**

Without a sealed explanatory field, genesis cannot be tracked immanently. If causal resources can be imported from outside the system under analysis \- a designer, a vital spark, a divine synchroniser \- then every apparent instance of structure-formation under constraint can be redescribed as intervention from elsewhere, and the analytic loses its grip. The problem is double: how to seal the field and furnish it with internal differentiation, without importing transcendence at either step. Spinoza provides the seal; Leibniz provides the furniture. Their joint failure \- a room that is airtight but frozen \- is the deficit the rest of the chapter exists to repair.

Axis I stages an oscillation between two structural numbers. Spinoza achieves σ \= 1: undifferentiated immanent closure in which substance is singular, modes are surface perturbations, and causal, energetic, and explanatory resources form a single sealed field \- but in which no genuine variation survives, because every modal distinction is absorbed back into the monism that permits it. Leibniz achieves σ \= ∞: infinite internal differentiation, every substance governed by its own unrepeatable law of the series, individuality secured at every level \- but in which coordination between individuals is guaranteed by a divine synchroniser external to the field, and encounter between substances is logically prohibited. The 1-∞ oscillation reveals that monism collapses difference while infinite multiplicity explodes coordination: neither pole, alone or in combination, can furnish the triadic minimum that compositional genesis demands. The sealed room lacks furniture at 1; the furnished room lacks genuine encounter at ∞; and at both poles burn rate is zero, because where there is no mediation there is nothing that must be thermodynamically maintained.

Re-Instantiation Protocol \- Axis I. The sodium acetate beaker is to this chapter what the planarian is to Ch. 1 and the Szilard engine to Ch. 2 (Ch. 3 §3.2.1). It returns twice in Axis I, each time exhibiting one new physical detail:

* §4.1.1 (Spinoza): immanent closure as calorimetric fact \- *new detail*: the enthalpy budget of the exothermic spike is fully accountable from internal free-energy gradients, with no residual requiring external supplementation.  
* §4.1.2 (Leibniz): the *lex seriei* as free-energy constraint-surface; the Signature of Defects \- *new detail*: the polymorphic underdetermination of the crystalline outcome under variable boundary conditions, and the crystal's unrepeatable defect distribution as individuality written in lattice topology.
