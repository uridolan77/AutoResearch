## **4.4.4 Disparation**

Valid intuition. A metastable system entails at least two heterogeneous orders, two regimes of magnitude or determination between which no direct communication yet occurs. Disparation is not mere quantitative difference or Hegelian opposition \- it is constitutive incompatibility between orders that cannot be satisfied simultaneously in a single homogeneous state.

Tested at the anchor. In the beaker, the thermal order and the chemical order are each internally coherent yet mutually incompatible: the thermal regime drives toward entropy maximisation, the chemical regime toward specific bond-angle satisfaction, and neither can absorb the other without remainder. The crystal lattice is neither the thermal state alone nor the chemical configuration alone \- it is a local resolution that integrates them by generating a structure in which the lowering of free energy and the satisfaction of specific bonding constraints coincide. \[Literal\]
