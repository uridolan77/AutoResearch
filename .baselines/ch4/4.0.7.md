## **4.0.7 The Ordeal**

Cool a sodium acetate solution past its equilibrium point without crystallising. It carries more solute than its phase diagram permits and yet stays liquid \- metastable, tensed, kinetically inhibited. Touch it with a seed crystal and the beaker flashes: an exothermic front races through the volume, precipitating a lattice that did not previously exist, releasing 264 kJ/kg of crystallisation enthalpy, exhausting a metastable surplus into determinate geometry. \[Literal\] The temperature spikes. Supersaturation collapses. Where there was a charged, undifferentiated solution there is now a crystal \- bounded, structured, irreversible \- and a depleted milieu that can never return to its pre-crystallisation state without external energy input.

This experiment is not an illustration of genesis. It is an ordeal \- the term Ch. 3 §3.2.1 installed \- that binds explanation to a single regime of description. The contract the ordeal enforces: no miracles, no tautologies. The new cannot be imported from outside the field's own resources, and it cannot already have been contained in the initial conditions under another name. Immanent closure means that whatever is said about individuation, value, or thought must be tested at the sodium acetate front or denied entry.

The antinomy of genesis \- either miracle (a rupture from outside the field) or tautology (a deduction of the new from what was already implicitly given) \- is not a problem the chapter will solve in the abstract and then apply to the beaker. It is a constraint the beaker imposes on the chapter's own operators. The ordeal is that demonstration. The crystal is the site where the metaphysical grammar of genesis will stand or fall.

What does the reader witness? Three structural functions, enacted simultaneously. *Variation*: the supersaturated solution's excess configurational degrees of freedom, the polymorphic underdetermination of the crystalline outcome. \[Literal\] *Encounter*: the seed crystal overpowering the dissolved state, each molecular lock-in as a resolution of competing energetic demands whose differential determines which regime prevails. \[Literal\] *Mediation*: the lattice geometry channelling subsequent incorporations, the propagating front's coherent advance, the sedimented regularity each resolved layer bequeaths to the next. \[Literal\] These three are the triadic minimum, established in Ch. 1 §1.3 and formally grounded in the Reduction Thesis. The chapter assembles this minimum by exapting eight philosophers \- extracting their structural contributions while refusing their metaphysical residues. The criterion of legitimacy is performance, not provenance: does the extracted operator bear its load at the sodium acetate front?

A named constraint governs every subsequent refusal. Any operator that performs selection, synchronisation, or governance must carry a thermodynamic receipt proportional to its informational work \- the Landauer Floor established in Ch. 2 §2.4. No receipt \- not in the ontology. An operator that governs at zero thermodynamic cost is a Demon-class error (Ch. 2 §2.8.7): it installs or maintains a distinction without entering it in the dissipative ledger. Wherever the genealogy subsequently refuses a residue \- geometric necessity, divine library, pre-established harmony, élan vital, Eternal Objects, Creativity, Apeironic preindividual, transcendental virtual \- the refusal cites the Landauer Floor and the Demon-class error taxonomy rather than re-deriving the logic from scratch.

Burn rate \- the rate of irreversible entropy production internal to the system, $dS\_i/dt \> 0$ \- names the intensity with which a closed system spends its metastable potential. But burn rate also names the cost of mediation: the thermodynamic expenditure required to maintain the constraint-architectures through which a field governs its own individuation. Wherever a constraint-regime persists, something is being spent to sustain it; wherever the spending ceases, the regime collapses \- the cessation-test of Ch. 3 §3.5.

The chapter tracks three regimes of burn rate, each keyed to the Stratogonic Principle's three conditions. *Zero*: a system whose gradients are fully spent, no historical transformation possible, no Stratogonic condition satisfied \- the frozen architecture of Axis I (Spinoza, Leibniz). *Positive but reversible*: the cycle of crisis and sedimentation operative, burn rate non-zero but constraint-closure not yet achieved \- each outcome capable of reversal, each product capable of un-happening \- the temporal agnosticism of Axis II (Peirce, Nietzsche). *Irreversibly positive*: each expenditure permanently altering the field's boundary conditions, stabilised aspect-selection achieved, constraint-closure approaching but compromised by residues \- the full temporal weight of Axes III and IV (Bergson, Whitehead, Simondon, Deleuze). The spiral traverses this progression, diagnosing each philosopher's structural number: which of the three compositional functions their apparatus secures, which it structurally cannot, and \- critically \- which of the three Stratogonic conditions the failure violates.
