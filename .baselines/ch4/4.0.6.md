## **4.0.6 Epistemic-Tier Marking**

Every claim in this chapter carries one of three epistemic-tier marks, matching the system installed in Ch. 1 §1.3.8 and Ch. 3 §3.5:

* \[Literal\] \- claims about the sodium acetate beaker, testable at the bench. Example: crystallisation enthalpy of 264 kJ/kg; polymorphic underdetermination between monoclinic and orthorhombic geometries.  
* \[Operationalisable\] \- claims about biological, developmental, and ecological cases where the triadic grammar can be operationalised through measurable proxies (metabolic rate, regulatory-network topology, morphogenetic field dynamics). These appear in the genealogy only as forward pointers; the operationalisation itself is the work of Chs. 5-7.  
* \[Structural-analogical\] \- claims about institutional, cognitive, and symbolic cases where the triadic grammar is deployed as a structural analogy whose cash-value depends on the operationalisation delivered in Chs. 8-10. These claims are explicitly marked and carry no probative weight until the downstream chapters pay the associated debt.
