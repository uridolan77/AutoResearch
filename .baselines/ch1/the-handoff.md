### **The Handoff**

Chapter 2 must deliver four results or the framework fails at its own strongest point:

1. A formal derivation of the Landauer bound as a physical constraint on any embedded discriminating system \- converting the cited result into the framework's audit floor.  
2. The quantitative burn-rate metric that operationalises the cessation-test in SI units across registers \- converting the qualitative distinction between flame and cell into a measurable threshold.  
3. The Stratogonic Principle \- the formal criterion for genuine stratification, specifying when a new register's macrovariables are non-constructible from the prior register's vocabulary.  
4. The formal proof that non-constructibility follows from the conjunction of the Landauer bound and the information-theoretic result \- closing the "in principle constructible" escape route.

The four deliverables are not independent: (1) grounds (2), (2) operationalises the threshold that (3) generalises, and (1) combined with the information-theoretic result jointly close (4). The derivation is a single argument with four outputs. The external convergences assembled in this chapter \- the Norton-Myrvold resolution, the Ismael self-reference argument, the Comolatti-Hoel cross-measure causal emergence result \- do not discharge the formal obligation; they clarify what the derivation must make precise and raise the stakes for its success.[^65]
