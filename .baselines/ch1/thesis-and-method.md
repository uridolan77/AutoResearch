### **Thesis and Method**

Three claims organise everything that follows; they are stated here as a map of the chapter's trajectory, exhibited and defended in 1.2–1.5. First, *persistence is priced*: any regime that maintains itself against the second law does so at a measurable rate of free-energy dissipation \- the burn rate \- and the rate is constitutive of the regime's identity, not incidental to it. Second, *cessation is diagnostic*: the order in which a regime's achievements unravel when funding stops maps the regime's organisational hierarchy without presupposing it. Third, *transduction is the general operator*: the irreversible resolution of metastable tension through which new constraint-identities precipitate is the common structure linking genesis, stabilisation, and stratification across registers. An operation is *priced* when any physically embedded system that performs it is constrained to dissipate a non-zero minimum of free energy under finite memory and time; an operation is *unpriced* when a framework invokes it without specifying those constraints. These two terms are the framework's master audit categories, and every subsequent section deploys them. The gradient of epistemic confidence introduced below (1.2, Epistemic Tiers) tracks which claims have been earned at which register and which remain owed.
